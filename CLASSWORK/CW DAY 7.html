Punjab


Punjab is famous for its vibrant culture, particularly its energetic folk music and dance forms like Bhangra, its rich and hearty cuisine, and its agricultural contributions to India.

Taj mahal


The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1631 by the fifth Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

Punjab

Punjab is a state in northwestern India, known as the "Granary of India" for its significant agricultural output. It is rich in history and culture, with a heritage that includes being the birthplace of Sikhism and a vibrant tradition of music, dance, and festivals like Baisakhi and Lohri. Geographically, the state is a plain with fertile land and is bordered by Pakistan to the west, and the Indian states of Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Haryana, and Rajasthan to its north, northeast, south, and southwest respectively.

Agra Fort


The Agra Fort, also known as the “Lal –Qila”, “Fort Rouge” or “Qila-i-Akbari”, is the highlight of the city of Agra, then capital of the Mughal Sultanate.
